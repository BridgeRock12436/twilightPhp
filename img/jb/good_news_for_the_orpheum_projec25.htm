Good news for the Orpheum Project

The Emails from Skip Pratt and Peter Finn are great news for the entire
Mountain Top community.
JBP

The Orpheum project is an example of a  public/private partnership that New York and
Greene County recognize as an important project supporting the economic development
of the mountaintop area. About 38% of the money needed for this $4 million project will
have come from public sources and some 62% from private donors.

The Catskill Mountain Foundation expects to start work again sometime in June and
work until completion.

Skip Pratt

And from Peter Finn:

Here are some things to pass on to donors to the Orpheum and others who are eagerly
 awaiting the resumption of work:

Chuck   Royce's   $500,000 donation was contingent on our firming up the rest of the funding needed
to complete the Orpheum.  While Senator Seward called me right after the NY State budget
was approved to tell me that he had obtained $500,000 for the Orpheum, the state's funding
wasn't actually official until last week when the Senate passed a resolution listing the specific
projects to be funded, with the amounts for each.  Attached is a copy of the list of projects,
listed alphabetically.  Our funding is listed under Catskill Mountain Foundation -
Orpheum Performing Arts Center.   The funding we now have in place to complete the
Orpheum is as follows:

a.    $500,000 from Senator Seward from the 2008 - 2009 NY State budget that was just passed,
b.    $500,000 from the Royce Family Foundation to be given as a match for the NY State funding
 just obtained,
c.    Just over $500,000 from the refinancing the Foundation is just completing with Key Bank
(this is on top of $500,000 in bank funding we have already
    invested in the Orpheum),
d.    Approximately $200,000 in outstanding pledges for the Orpheum
e.    $30,000 from the Greene County Legislature being given to the Catskill Mountain Foundation in
2008 for the Orpheum theater, and all future grants from the Greene     County Legislature to CMF
in the future up to a total of $250,000.
e.    $50,000 from the NY State grant that the Hunter Foundation got for the Orpheum
g.    $7,500 of façade money that Greene County Economic Development has awarded to the
Catskill Mountain Foundation for the Orpheum

The fact that the NY State funding for the Orpheum was not finalized until last week (and worry
that the state's precarious budget situation could cause this funding to be changed before it was finalized)
caused a delay in our plan to commit to our subcontractors to a May start date for resumption of work
at the Orpheum.  We needed to know that the funds would actually be in hand to pay the subcontractors
before we committed to them.  While we still don't have any of the new funding in hand, we are expecting
it shortly and are now working to book our subcontractors to start work again as soon as possible. 
We expect work to resume at the Orpheum at some point in June. 

Peter Finn
