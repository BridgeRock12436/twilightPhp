Twilight Park Artists  61th Annual  Art Exhibition August 9and August 10, 2008 

The Exhibition featured over 200  works by artists, sculptors, and photographers in addition to children’s art from the Mountain Top,
 and surrounding  areas. As in most recent shows, the Co-Chairman, Gracia Dayton and Helen Price hosted a guided tour of the
show  quoting statements made about the pieces receiving awards by the shows' judges. The opening reception on August 9th  at the Twilight Park Clubhouse was attended by more than three hundred and fifty people.

Judges for the shows prizes for this year’s show were :

·        John R. Stomberg, Deputy Director and Senior Curator for the judges Exhibitions at the Williams College Museum of Art,  Stomberg holds a BA in Art History from Georgetown University and both MA and Ph.D degrees from Boston University. He has taught at Boston University, Northeastern University, the University of Massachusetts (Boston).

·        Megan  Flaherty, a staff member since May of 2007 at the Center for Photography at Woodstock, NY. received a BA in Visual and Cultural Studies from Prescott College in Prescott, AZ. Flaherty is a recipient of the Frederick and Frances Sommer Fellowship.

 The judges presented blue ribbons for the Judges Choice; red ribbons for an Award of Excellence; green ribbons for Merit Awards and white ribbons for Honorable Mention and one gold ribbon for Best in Show.

  On Sunday August 10 at 3:00pm noted printmaker Scott W. Parker, from New York City and “Award of Excellence” winner in the 2007 Twilight Show, gave a demonstration of his printmaking techniques using linoleum blocks. The judges for last year’s Award of Excellence Prize” for Parker’s lino print noted “excellent use of positive and negative space, successful use of black and white, marking, and masterful use of the medium”.

A classical and original piano music was provided as well by Michael Wedd and brother Raphael of Philmont, NY.

The 61th Twilight Art Show was sponsored by Twilight Park Artists and residents of Twilight Park, a community established “on the mountain top” in Haines Falls, NY in 1888. The show was co-chaired by Gracia and Bruce Dayton.  Members of the art committee included Ann Collins, Bruce Dayton,  Hendy Dayton,  Susan Ferris, Mary Gaillard, Sandra Gooder, Olivia Mechlowitz, Helen Burdon Price, Rachel Roll, Rena Tarnowski, Catherine Tangey and Heather Wilson.
