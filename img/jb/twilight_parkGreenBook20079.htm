Introduction to the Green Book - 2005

Since it has not been updated for a number of years, Jean Barish Sterrett and others have prepared this revision of the Twilight Park Green Book. It was approved by the Board of Directors at their July 2, 2005 meeting. In addition to a hard-copy distribution, it is posted on the Twilight Park Website (www.TwilightPark.com).

The Twilight Park Green Book is intended to provide both as an introduction for newcomers and a reference for members. Its purpose is to outline services, rules and policies applicable to the members and visitors to Twilight Park. The relationship of the Green Book to other sources of information is more fully described in the section on Governance. This Green Book is an evolving document and an attempt will be made to provide you with new pages as changes are made.

Because changes in rules and polices are frequently made in response to changing circumstances, it is impractical to keep the hard-copy version currently updated. Consequently, the official and authoritative version of the Green Book is the Website version.

Importance of Volunteers

Much of the actual work of providing the support, the many services and activities, and the functioning of Twilight Park is done by volunteers. For the well-being of the Park, it is vital that Cottagers volunteer to undertake activities designed to enhance community life and are willing to serve on committees and the like. All are urged, encouraged, and even expected to participate in whatever form most suits their talents.

A Short History of Twilight Park

Twilight Park has its roots in the "Twilight Club" founded in New York in 1883 as a dinner and discussion group. The club still exists, in a somewhat modified form, but no longer has any connection to Twilight Park.

Charles Wingate, the founder of the Twilight Club, was also the founder of Twilight Park. A "sheep pasture" to the south of the falls was purchased for $10 an acre in 1887 to establish a summer community for club members. The Twilight Park Association was formed in 1888. Similar communities were formed about this time in Onteora and Elka Park.

By 1890 there were 30 cottages. Several inns were built, and some rebuilt when they burned down. Only Santa Cruz Inn remains as the present Pratt cottage. A tennis court was built in 1889 and a tennis building added in 1890, to include four bowling alleys and a pool table. The golf course was also added in 1889 and the swimming pool in 1921. The present Clubhouse is from 1932. Hikers joined the "Lingernots" and could earn a diploma in Latin as Doctor of Pedistry by walking 300 miles and climbing 20 mountains in a single season. Churches built in Twilight include St. Paul’s in 1891, the Union Chapel in 1897 and the present All Angels in 1896.

Further information is available in John MacGahan’s excellent history Twilight Park - The First Hundred Years.

Governance

 

Twilight Park is a community more than one hundred years old that is governed by a variety of rules and traditions. Chief among these are the Lease Agreement, the By-laws, and rules established by the members at the Annual Meeting and the Board as set forth in this Green Book.

Lease Agreement

The Lease Agreement provides for certain contractual rights, privileges, responsibilities and obligations between Cottagers and the Park. There have been many amendments to this agreement over the years, so one Cottager’s agreement may vary slightly from a neighbor’s. All Leases provide that Cottagers shall be bound by rules established by the Park and its Board of Directors.

By-laws

The By-laws contain the basic procedures for the governance of the Park by a Board of Directors. Directors are elected for up to two three-year terms. They in turn elect a President and Vice-President from among their members, and also a Secretary, Treasurer, and Assistant Treasurer, who do not need to be members of the Board.

The Board manages the affairs of the Park including fixing assessments, making and enforcing rules for conduct and admission to membership in the Park. Board members represent Cottagers’ interests and are available to discuss any issues or concerns.

Under the By-laws, the Board has established a number of committees. The largest and most prominent of these is the Activities Committee, which, through its many sub-committees, sponsors the great variety of activities that we all enjoy. Many of the policies stated here were first developed by the respective committees and then approved by the Board.

Annual Meeting

On the first Saturday in August, the Annual Cottagers Meeting is held for the election of directors and such other business as may come before the membership. While not required by Park rules or the By-laws, as a matter of practice the annual budget for the next calendar year is approved and individual committee reports are presented.

Green Book

This Green Book sets forth Park rules approved by the members at the Annual Meeting and by the Board and provides a handy guide for new and old Cottagers. It is supplemented by the annual calendar, which contains not only a calendar of meetings and activities but also considerable information on members, maps, committees and local services. Additional information is available in the Owl, which is published during the summer, the Snowy Owl, published during the Spring, and on the Twilight Park Website.

Services & Recreation

SUPERVISION – The Superintendent looks out for your property when you are not in Twilight. It is important to notify him of your arrival and departure times and to leave your cottage securely locked when you are away. He must be informed if the cottage is to be used by others – adult children, family or friends. He must have two keys to your cottage. One is for his own use and the other is to be used, only when you have given permission, by housekeepers or workmen needing access. The Superintendent must be notified if your lock is changed.

WATER - Usually turned on about May 15 and turned off about October 15. The Association is responsible for "delivering" water to your house, but hook-up and draining of the plumbing must be arranged by you with a local plumber.

SEWER – The Park Sewage Disposal System also operates from about May 15 to October 15. To keep the facility working and to avoid costly repairs it is imperative that you do not discard non-biologically degradable materials such as plastics, cotton, glass or metallic items into the sewer.

Cottagers planning off-season use (October 15 to May 15) should refer to the Winter Use guidelines on page 13 and to the paragraph on well drilling and individual winter sewer systems on page 9.

GARBAGE – From about May 15 to October 15, the garbage collector is under contract to the Park to pick up household trash and garbage from each cottage without charge to the Cottagers. For the first month the garbage is picked up only on Tuesdays. For the last two weeks in June until the first Tuesday in September, the garbage is picked up on Tuesdays and Saturdays. Starting with the first Tuesday in September until the third Tuesday in October, the garbage is picked up only on Tuesdays. Each Cottager is entitled to the pickup of one load of burnable lawn refuse each year (it does not have to be on Earth Day). During the rest of the year, all collection is at the Cottager’s expense.

A few simple rules:

Place refuse in securely tied sturdy plastic bags and store bags for pick-up (preferably in a raccoon- and bear-proof shed or container) near your cottage, not on the road.

To conform to the Waste Recycling Program, newspapers, corrugated cardboard, glass bottles and certain plastic containers must be separated for collection.

For off-season collection and the pick-up of large or unusual items or more details on recycling, contact Bruce Feml (589-6155).

DUMPING – Disposal of garbage, brush or tree cuttings, or any other form of rubbish within the Park is strictly prohibited. Arrangements to dispose of garbage and rubbish can be made through Bruce Feml. The firm employed to cut brush or trees should remove the cuttings. As a special one-time service each year, the Park truck will pick up and dispose of one load of brush cuttings, dead branches and other such outdoor rubbish if it is piled neatly by the side of the road.

LABOR – Requests for work by Park employees (such as wood delivery, mowing, spring clean-ups, etc.) must be made to the Superintendent.

FIREWOOD – May be ordered from the Superintendent. No Saturday or Sunday deliveries. Winter needs must be ordered by November 1.

AUTOMATIC GATES – The gates are operated by codes punched in on the keypad. Codes may be obtained from the Real Estate Representative.

GATEHOUSE – Please notify the person staffing the gatehouse of expected guests (589-5820) or ask guests to call your cottage from the gatehouse.

INTRA-PARK MAIL – May be delivered to and collected from the gatehouse’s gatekeeper from July 1 through Labor Day. During the off-season, mail is distributed in the winter mailboxes in the Superintendent’s outer office. All winter mail not picked up as of July 1 is transferred to the gatehouse mailboxes.

PARK CALENDAR – Lists upcoming events of the season, emergency and commercial telephone numbers as well as phone numbers of Cottagers and Park buildings. Calendars are made available on Earth Day or can be obtained at the Superintendent’s office.

DAY CAMP – Children of Cottagers, renters, guests are welcome. For information, contact the Camp Committee Chairperson. Applications are mailed in the Spring.

TENNIS – Courts are available to Twilighters and their guests on a first-come basis with the teaching court the province of the pro. On weekends and holidays, tennis whites are required, children must defer to adults and players are asked not to play longer than an hour if others are waiting. Appropriate tennis wear, including tennis shoes, is required at all times. Lowered nets may be raised only by permission of the Tennis Chairperson or the Superintendent. Additional rules and regulations are developed by the Tennis Committee and posted at the courts.

SWIMMING POOL – Please introduce your family and guests to the lifeguards and familiarize yourself with the pool’s safety rules. Children under eleven must be accompanied by an adult. Schedule of hours when the pool is open and the lifeguard on duty is posted at the Gatehouse and Pool House. Additional rules and regulations are developed by the Swimming Pool Committee and posted at the pool.

FISHING – Fishing may be available at the reservoir. It is occasionally stocked with trout for the Park community, so please limit your catch.

HIKING – Speak to the Chairperson of the Linger-Not Society for information and suggestions about trails and scenic trips.

PARK SERVICES – Lawn services, cleaning of gutters, shoveling of paths, plowing of driveways, etc., are available upon request to the Superintendent at hourly rates.

WEB SITE – The Webmaster maintains a website for use by Twilight Park members. Items of general interest are posted regularly. Contact the Webmaster if you have information that may be appropriate and it will be posted for you. A logon name and password is necessary to access the site. Please be careful to see that these are not made distributed to anyone except Park members and their family members.

DIRECTORY – Also on the Web is a directory for members and families. Information posted there is the source for a variety of uses, including billing, the annual Holiday address list, e-mail notices and the Park calendar. Under the Reports / E-mail Reports pages is an "EMAIL ALL" facility to send a message to all those listed in the directory. Guidelines for appropriate usage of this facility are included there. A separate User Id and Logon is needed to access the directory. You can also use the site to maintain entries for you and your family members.

RULES AND THEIR ENFORCEMENT - Below, and throughout the Greenbook, there are a number of Park rules that have been adopted from time to time by the Membership or the Board of Directors. There are also rules in the lease. As noted in the Governance section, the lease provides that all Cottagers are bound by rules established by the Park or its Board of Directors. Each leaseholder/cottager shall be responsible for the actions of members of their families, guests and invitees for compliance with all of the rules and regulations of Twilight Park whether or not in active residence in Twilight Park. While the rules below do not provide for enforcement remedies, the Board of Directors has the authority to impose such remedies as it determines appropriate. For most of these rules, enforcement is carried out simply by asking those who have breached them to do so no longer. For more serious infractions, more material remedies may be imposed.

The lease provides in part that "No disorderly or disreputable persons shall be allowed to occupy the demised premises or any part thereof." Disorderly behavior includes any conduct which endangers or is injurious to the welfare of other leaseholders, cottagers, members of their family, guests or invitees or to property situated in Twilight Park. A violation of this proscription against disorderly behavior shall be subject to disciplinary action as determined by the Board of Directors. Such disciplinary action may include:

a fine(s), to be added as an additional line to a cottager/leaseholder’s quarterly bill;

a suspension of privileges for the offending individual(s) to utilize Twilight Park facilities (clubhouse, pool, tennis courts, etc); or

such other disciplinary action as the Board determines.

SPEED LIMIT – 20 miles an hour – or less if driving conditions warrant. Children playing or bicycling, walkers enjoying Twilight’s natural beauty or just stopping to chat always have the right of way in the Park! So please obey "One Way," "Slow" and "No Parking" signs. When parking your car, remember the roads must be kept clear in case of fire or other emergency. Please ask your guests to follow these rules.

VEHICLES – The roads and land within Twilight are private, but we require that any motorized vehicle (including motorbikes and motorcycles) bear an effective license plate and be operated by a properly licensed driver. Except for the dirt road along the creek, all motor vehicles, except Park maintenance vehicles, are banned from the golf course year round.

RENTALS AND SALES – Must be negotiated through the official Board Real Estate Representative, as outlined in the Real Estate Procedures and Membership sections.

WELL-DRILLING AND INDIVIDUAL WINTER SEWER SYSTEMS – This may include the short-term use of a chemical toilet as outlined on page 13. The installation of long term facilities requires Board approval, based on a satisfactory engineering survey and the issuance of a Health Department permit (generally limited to off-season use only). Please discuss with the Superintendent or the chair of the Water and Sewer Committee before undertaking site evaluation or construction.

CONSTRUCTION AND EXTERNAL RENOVATION – As stated in the By-Laws, to preserve and protect the natural beauty and character of Twilight Park, plans for new buildings or any alterations to existing buildings and other changes to cottages, property and landscaping must be approved by the Board or the Design Review Committee before work is begun.

For additional information on when and how to obtain approval, see the Changes to Cottages and Property section of this handbook.

Contractors working on your property must abide by the following rules:

Work may not begin before 8 a.m. on weekdays during July and August; and

No work may be done on weekends during July and August or on Memorial and Labor Day weekends.

The Board also works through the Design Review Committee to correct unpleasant sights and sounds which may occur in the Park – unconcealed garbage cans, visible clotheslines, towels and sleeping bags on railings, dead branches, toys or bicycles left on the road – and unnecessary noise. To obviate the need for any action, we simply ask every Cottager to always be considerate of his or her neighbors in the time-honored Twilight way.

CLUBHOUSE – For adult casual use of the Clubhouse (requires at least one adult over 21 for supervision), such as watching television in the Lounge or using the Xerox machine, keys can be obtained from the key box in the Superintendent’s outer office. Before taking the keys, the user must sign the sign-out sheet. In leaving the Clubhouse, all lights must be turned off and doors locked and the keys must be returned to key box in the Superintendent’s outer office. Children should not be allowed to roam behind the stage, in the kitchen area or in the loft. Everyone should be out of the Clubhouse by 10 p.m. Exceptions will be granted by the Clubhouse Chair (whose identity is set out in the Calendar under Activities Committees) in special circumstances. If a Cottager would like to use the Clubhouse for a private event, s/he should contact the Clubhouse Chair at least one month before the event to find out about reserving the space, possible fees, insurance requirements and disallowed activities that can subject the Park to liability claims.

FIRE – Always be cautious. For your protection a good-sized fire extinguisher is suggested – preferably a five-pound dry chemical type, to resist freezing in winter. It should be readily available and your household instructed in its proper use. Each fire extinguisher should be checked each year and refreshed per the manufacturer’s recommendations. Formulating an effective plan for leaving your cottage in case of fire is a wise precaution. Burning of anything – leaves, brush or trash – is prohibited.

Smoke detectors are strongly recommended on every floor and in every bedroom.

CAMPFIRES – Campfires, holiday bonfires and cookouts are not permitted in the Park, except at Wingate Field and on the old golf course. Permission of the Park superintendent is required and only designated areas are to be used for such fires.

CHIMNEYS – Should be cleaned at least once a year.

FIREARMS & FIREWORKS – Hunting or discharging of any kind of any firearm is strictly prohibited anywhere in Twilight.  Similarly, any use of fireworks, other than sparklers, is prohibited within the Park, except as may be authorized for celebrations by the Board of Directors.

HAINES FALLS - The falls at the entrance to the Park has been the site of tragic accidents.  Walking anywhere below the bridge or near the falls is strictly forbidden.  Cottagers are to request anyone found there to leave. This restriction applies to Cottagers and their guests. If they fail to leave, the Superintendent should be notified, and if he is not available, the State Police.

 

DOGS – Dogs should be kept under control at all times and are not permitted at the swimming pool, tennis courts or in the upper reservoir. Dogs roaming loose should be reported to the Board. Owners will receive notice of a $10 fine along with their property bill. For repeated, or more serious offenders, a fine of $50 will be levied. Also, Cottagers should be considerate of barking by their dogs, especially when this barking occurs when the dogs are left alone, and the owners may not be sufficiently aware of the nuisance caused for their neighbors. Dogs may run free at the old golf course, but must be under the control of their masters.

OFF-SEASON – See Winter Use.

CLOSING THE COTTAGE – The following procedures are suggested:

Water – Make arrangements with your plumber before you leave and inform him of washers, dishwashers, etc., which should be serviced, in addition to disconnecting and draining your plumbing.

Sewer – Be sure your plumber knows the sewer system does not operate after mid-October.

Gas – It is recommended that your gas supply be turned off at the tanks.

Electric – Turn off the main electric power switch and notify the power company, if needed.

Phone – Service can be temporarily discontinued.

Lock–up – Check all doors and windows carefully and notify the Superintendent of your departure.

BEARS -  Roaming bears can be a hazard in the Park.   Procedures promulgated
by the New York State Department of Environment Protection should be followed. 
In particular, the feeding of bears is illegal. Also prohibited is the use of
outdoor bird feeders with bird seed.  Currently, these are specifically
prohibited in the Park.

 

Changes to Cottages and Property

Twilight has a Design Review Committee to help maintain the beauty of the Park. Cottagers wishing to make changes to the exterior of their cottages or leaseholds must first receive written permission from the Design Review Committee. In accordance with the By-laws, all new buildings or additions to existing buildings must be approved by the Board, on recommendation by the Design Review Committee, before work can begin.

The changes to cottages or property that require approval include:

New buildings

Additions and exterior renovations to existing buildings

Changes to color of exterior painting

Window renovations

Tree removal > 8 inches in diameter (including dead and dying trees). Tree cutting that is noisy during July and August and the Memorial and Labor Day Weekends is subject to the same time-restriction rules (set out on page 9) as contractors working on your property.

Driveways

Landscape lighting and major exterior lighting

Sewage system changes

Major landscaping that would alter the view from Park lands and neighboring cottages

Other changes to cottages or cottage land that could affect the character of the Park (if there is any question, please contact a member of the Design Review Committee to discuss whether approval is required).

The steps to be taken to secure approval from the Committee:

Complete an Application form which can be obtained from the Superintendent. When applicable, plans or sketches, color samples or brochures of products must also be submitted.

Contact a member of the Design Review Committee and all neighbors within sight of the property and forward copies of plans to all.

Review plans (for construction use architectural renderings and plans, to scale) and conduct walk-throughs as necessary to secure written approval first from all neighbors, followed by written approval from the Design Review Committee. In cases where a neighbor objects, the Cottager and neighbor should do everything reasonable to reach a compromise. (In rare cases, the Design Review Committee may give approval without full neighbor consent.)

Forward completed form (including all plans, written comments and approvals) to:

The Board of Directors – for new and major construction. The Board will forward to Superintendent.

Superintendent for all other changes.

Winter Use

The occupancy of cottages during the off season* is limited to those cottages suitably equipped with acceptable sewer disposal facilities (as approved by the Board). Unauthorized occupancy of cottages shall be subject to administrative punitive action including fines of $100 for the first night and $200 per night thereafter. This board resolution is to protect the Park sewer system and possible loss of the State Pollutant Discharge Elimination System (SPDES) permit.

*Usually October 15th to May 15th, or as set annually by the Board.

Cottagers contemplating winter use may meet the requirements for a satisfactory sewage treatment facility by a number of different systems which have been approved by the Board on recommendation from the Chair of the Water and Sewer Committee. They include chemical toilets, electrical toilets, aerobic systems with dedicated sewer lines, holding tanks and other such systems. Please be advised that the system must be approved in advance of occupancy by the Superintendent who will supply information about the requirements of any particular system.

In addition to sewage disposal, there are a number of other considerations to bear in mind for winter use of cottages in Twilight. The following Winter Rules apply:

If you need parking, advance notification is required since this cannot be done after the snow is piled deep. You cannot park in the roads, so clearing a space is essential.

Please let the Superintendent know by telephone, letter or e-mail (address found in the Calendar on the page with telephone listings) in advance of every intended use of your cottage. If use is to be by friends, children or family, notification must be given by letter and specific dates should be stated. And for your own convenience, a telephone call to him requesting snow and weather conditions can save you considerable trouble.

When you arrive in the Park, blow your horn as you go by the Superintendent’s cottage regardless of the time of day or night.

All secondary roads – Church, Barkeloo and Pharmacy Hill - will be closed throughout the winter. It is also possible that the automatic gate will be out of service from time to time.

If you are in the Park and observe any person on the Falls, you must require that they leave at once. This restriction applies to Cottagers and their guests as well. Should the person refuse to leave, the Superintendent should be called, and if he is not available, the State Police.

The following hints may be helpful:

Use snow tires. You may need chains and sand to reach the upper level. Please remember that the Superintendent is not expected to pull you out when stuck. If you get stuck, call Haines Falls Auto Towing: 589-6181.

Continual use of fireplaces and wood stoves increase the danger of chimney fires. Be sure yours are clean and in good condition.

There is no free trash pickup service off season. Please make your own arrangements with Bruce Feml: 589-6155.

Please place orders for firewood before November 1, partly because of the possibility of snow, after which the work crews are no longer available to deliver wood.

For the various reasons listed above, deliveries of furniture, appliances, etc., should not be made between November 1 and May 1.

Please keep your brush and other clippings away from your cottage and particularly away from road culverts. This measure will keep run-off water flowing properly and will prevent diversion of water with damage to roads and to your property. Please notify the Superintendent of any large amounts of brush so that he can make arrangements for disposal for which you will be billed.

Park water lines are cleansed with a chlorine solution each Spring. Please clear the water lines in your cottage before using the water within the system for the first time each season.

Please observe these suggestions and recommendations on sewage and winter use. This will enable the Park to prevent damage and keep our (your) maintenance costs down.

Annual Assessment

The expenditures made to operate and maintain the Park’s facilities are funded by an annual assessment charged to each cottage. The assessment amount is determined by the Board of Directors. It covers a calendar year (with installments payable on the first day of February, May, August and November) and has three components: property charge, operating assessment and capital contribution.

The property portion of the assessment represents the lease payment for the lots associated with each cottage. The current charge is a $50 minimum payment on the first lot and then $10 per additional lot per year and, accordingly, the aggregate lease payment varies from cottage to cottage. The range of payments is from the $50 minimum to approximately $150 per cottage per year. This charge is made once a year and appears on the first bill of the year (February).

The operating portion of the assessment covers the routine cost of running and maintaining the Park. These expenditures include salaries of Park personnel, maintenance of grounds, buildings and equipment, activities (pool, tennis, etc.) and general expenses such as property taxes, insurance, garbage removal, etc.

The capital portion of the assessment is used to fund the cost of new equipment or major enhancements to Park structures and facilities. Such expenditures, because of their magnitude, are capitalized and depreciated over a number of years rather than expensed in a single year. Recent examples of such expenditures are the upgraded water system; improvements to the Clubhouse facilities; and the purchase of a new dump truck.

Assessment payments are due quarterly on the 1st of the month as stated on the bill. A penalty equal to 10% of any quarterly amount due is automatically charged if payment is not received within two months of the due date. We regret that Park water and sewer service cannot be restored to the cottage until all delinquent bills and fine are paid in full.

 

Real Estate Procedures

All transactions, either sale, transfer or rental, must be handled through the Board’s Marketing Representative. As mandated by the Board of Directors, no offer can be considered valid until it is submitted to the Marketing Representative (Rep).

Selling

The Cottager signs a LISTING form obtained from the Rep and remits the listing fee of $500 to Twilight Cottagers. The fee is non-refundable.

The Rep prepares an advertisement for the sale of the cottage and mails marketing information to the appropriate constituencies of current and past Cottagers, friends of Cottagers and renters.

The Rep receives bids and communicates bids to the Seller.

Upon acceptance of a bid, the Rep will prepare an AGREEMENT OF SALE binding the transfer between Seller and Buyer for three (3) months and subject to the rules and regulations of THE TWILIGHT PARK ASSOCIATION to the transfer of the lease.

In accordance with the requirements provided by the Membership Policy section of this Green Book, the prospective Buyer must be approved by the Membership Committee for Cottage ownership, and have previously rented a cottage in Twilight Park.

The prospective Buyer must also submit a confidential credit rating report to the Chair of the Membership Committee or the Chair’s delegate.

The Seller has the right to sell the Cottage to whomever the Seller wishes, subject to the Buyer being approved by the Membership Committee and the Chair of the Finance Committee.

If the Buyer seeks to procure a mortgage for the purchase of the cottage, the terms of the mortgage must include a Letter of Recognition. In the Letter of Recognition the Bank, the Buyer and the Twilight Park Association agree that in the event of default the Bank will notify the Association and the Association will have the sole and priority right to cure the default or to purchase the leasehold.

The principals will decide upon a closing date and the Rep will prepare a closing statement which will include adjustments for assessments and taxes, fees, final costs and any other appropriate contingencies.

At closing the Rep will issue a SURRENDER OF LEASE, ACCEPTANCE OF SURRENDER and issue a new ninety-nine year LEASE. The Rep will prepare appropriate tax forms.

The Rep will record the lease at the Greene County Courthouse, and keep a copy of the lease on file in the Twilight Park Deeds File.

Renting

Cottagers wishing to rent their cottage contact the Rep. The Rep prepares a Rental Availability Schedule and mails it to Cottagers, former tenants and prospects. The Twilight Park Website posts the Rental Availability Schedule.

The Rep shows cottages to prospective Renters and serves as a liaison between Cottager and prospective renters.

Prospective Renters will be advised that they must be approved for membership in the Park before they can rent a cottage, in accordance with the provisions of the Membership Policy.

The Rep prepares a sub-lease for the rental. This can be done through the mail.

New Cottages on Unleased Parcels

Prospective Buyers will be advised that they must be approved for membership in the Park before they can build a cottage, in accordance with the provisions of the Membership Policy, including the provision for a previous rental in the Park.

In the granting of a lease on a Park-controlled buildable Lot – within the boundaries of the currently built area of the Park – to a prospective Cottager who proposes to construct a new cottage, the following conditions will apply:

A non-refundable fee of $5000 shall be immediately due and payable on the issuance of a lease.

The lessee shall provide the Design Review Committee with all architectural plans within one year from the date of the lease. Failure to do so will result in forfeiture of the lease.

The Board will receive a recommendation from the Design Review Committee regarding the plans and then review the plans and provide its approval or request for revisions within three months.

The lessee shall commence construction of the approved Cottage within two years of the date of the lease.

The lessee shall commence to pay a full assessment three years from the date of the lease.

The Buyer will be responsible for 25% of the annual membership dues, inclusive of the portion that is classified as the Capital Assessment, in effect at the date of the lease. This shall be payable from the beginning of the lease.

All taxes of any kind leviable on the leased parcel shall be the obligation of the lessee from the date of the lease.

Fees

The following fees are applicable to Real Estate transactions:

Listing of Cottage for sale: $500.

Transfer of Lease to spouse, between co-owners of a lease or to a direct descendent: $100 plus filing fees.

Transfer of a Lease to other than the above: Five percent (5%) of the selling price or one annual assessment, whichever is larger, paid by Seller.

Transfer of a Cottage-controlled, non-built parcel: six percent (6%).

Guidelines Adopted by the Board of Directors

 

(This section of the Greenbook contains two statements of Guidelines that the Board has adopted in order to provide it and future boards with a framework for addressing questions regarding the construction of new cottages and disputes between cottagers. The Board believes that publicizing these guidelines may help cottagers, when confronting such issues, have a better understanding of how their Board may respond.)

 

I. New Residential Construction and Additions/Modifications

Purpose

Twilight Park is an historic community and we seek to preserve those aspects of architectural environment that contribute to our unique character, heritage and special qualities. To achieve this objective, the Design and Review Committee reviews proposal on a case-by-case basis. The following architectural Guidelines have been created for those who wish to build within Twilight Park

Guidelines

Guidelines for new construction will encourage utilization of design elements and materials that are common in Twilight’s historic architectural styles.

The Park works through the Board and Design and Review Committee to review and approve all new residential buildings, exterior modifications and additions. (See Process for Changes to Cottages and Properties in the first part of the Green Book).

Neighboring cottages must approve all plans. The Design and Review Committee will pay particular attention to:

1. Site Planning

Setback – conformance to the normal front, side and rear yard setbacks in a way that is aesthetically pleasing to the area.

Views – respect for neighbors and community views.

Parking and Vehicle Access – off street access whenever possible to ensure clear access to emergency vehicles.

Environment – respect for natural surroundings and functional issues such as water run-off.

Utility/Mechanical Areas – located as inconspicuously as possible, possibly to the rear of the building. Invisible from the road and landscaped, if possible.

2. Tear Downs

Tearing down buildings of historic or architectural significance is strongly discouraged. Exceptions will be considered by the Board, based on D&R recommendation.

3. Scale

Plans for new constructions must take into consideration situation on lot, proximity to neighbors and impact on the environment.

4. Architectural Style and Design

Twilight cottages were largely built in the late 1800s with a predominance of Queen Anne Style. A professional architect should be used who is knowledgeable of and sympathetic to these aims and uses historic elements and quality materials common to the houses of Twilight, e.g.

Natural Materials – Clapboard, Shingle, Bark siding

Gable (pitched) Roof

Inset Porches

Cross Gables

Multiple Windows with Decorative Enhancement

Latticed Foundations

Upstairs Sleeping Porches

2+ Floors

Historic paint colors

5. Landscaping

Appropriate to the design and style of Twilight. Includes all visible elements –

Trees, Bushes, Plants and Ground Covering

Decorative and Limited Use of Stone Walls (in keeping with local character and materials)

Retaining walls – Local Stone (with aesthetically pleasing placement and/or landscaping)

Landscape Lighting to maintain Twilight’s character and reduce ambient light

Outdoor Furniture and Recreational Items.

6. Additions and Modifications

The Guidelines above will apply as is relevant to additions and modifications. Original materials and features that are consistent with good Twilight design and quality should be kept. Deteriorated architectural features, including windows, should be repaired rather then replaced.

7. Blueprints

Architectural blueprints are required for all construction. Architectural drawings will be acceptable for alterations or small additions to existing cottages.

 

Additional Guidance

The D&R Committee will not approve the following design features –

1. Vinyl siding, prefabricated stone foundation siding or foam core stucco exteriors

2. Concrete or raw sheet metal chimneys

3. Replacing windows of architectural merit (such as diamond-paned windows) with prefabricated, vinyl windows

4. Interlocking brick or railroad ties for retaining walls on property that is visible to neighbors or to the street

5. Unpainted wood or plastic storage sheds

6. Exterior paint colors and stains that are exceptionally bright

7. Property line fencing of any kind

8. Landscape lighting plans that shed an excess amount of ambient light

9. Recreational play sets containing bright colors or plastic and are visible to neighbors and

10. Decks with visible underpinnings or railings that are inconsistent with the style of the cottage.

Application of Guidelines

The Guidelines may be changed from time to time to reflect the need to maintain the desirable character of Twilight Park.

In addition to performing a formal review of building permit applications, the D&R encourages those who are considering construction projects in the Park to consult informally with D&R members at any time.

Design Changes

The Guidelines were created for the benefit of current and prospective cottage owners contemplating new residential buildings, exterior modifications and additions within Twilight Park. Any plans for such new residential buildings, exterior modifications and additions must be submitted for formal approval by the Design and Review Committee and, as appropriate, the Board of Directors, in accordance with the Process for Changes to Cottages and Properties in the Green Book.

Owners will be responsible for compliance with the formally approved plans only, and any changes or modifications to the approved plans must be submitted to the Design and Review Committee and, as appropriate, the Board of Directors, for consideration. Failure to comply with the approved plans will result in mandated remedies at owners’ expense.

 

II. Twilight Park Private Dispute Resolution

 

When a cottager/cottager dispute arises involving a tangible (e.g., land) or intangible (e.g., views, quality of life) asset or other property of the Park, where a resolution is not provided by existing Park rules (hereinafter defined as a "Private Dispute"), there is a strong presumption that the Board of Directors should abstain from resolving such disputes.

Background

Disputes arise in many forms and the Board of Directors should retain flexibility to handle the specifics of each situation as it sees fit. Clearly, if a dispute involves the enforcement of existing Park rules as established in the By-Laws, the Proprietary Lease or Park rules, then such a dispute is no longer a private matter between cottagers, and the Board may act within its powers and in accordance with existing rules to enforce such rules. This type of issue is not considered a "Private Dispute" among cottagers and it is not the subject of this discussion.

Reasons for Abstention

First, Litigation Risk to Twilight: To the extent the Board becomes a party in any dispute and in potential litigation between cottagers, the Community as a whole is exposed to legal and financial risk. The Board’s priority must be to protect the overall Park from all risk, including legal liability.

Second, Unfair Role for Board Members: The Twilight Board is a group of volunteers drawn from a relatively small community. Asking members to get involved in resolving an issue where there may be no good objective criteria for resolution puts Board members in a difficult and even unfair position and may discourage future Board candidates.

Third, the notion of an "ultimate authority" to resolve disputes is against the traditional spirit of Twilight: Twilight has a history of neighborly support (Charles Wingate called it "enlightened self-interest") and we want to encourage neighbors to work out their differences among themselves. The Board Members, as leaders and representatives of the Twilight community, should advance the community spirit by trying to solve disputes amicably vs. adjudicating them. Setting up a permanent body of people and rules may in fact encourage people to resort to this alternative instead of addressing issues in the spirit of Twilight that was so eloquently described by a number of cottagers at the August 2004 cottagers meeting.

 

 

 

Recommendations

If the Board concludes that a Private Dispute is material to the Park in some way, and the cottagers involved are unable to privately reach a solution, the Board may decide on its own terms to take any number of actions, including:

1. Offering its or its Members’ influence, persuasion, services, etc., to resolve the Private Dispute informally. The Board was extremely active in this manner in the past.

2. Offering to help establish a mediation procedure if the dispute cannot be settled through bilateral negotiation. Mediation fees, costs, and expenses would be borne equally by the disputants. Each disputant will also bear the fees and expenses of its own counsel and expert witnesses, and all costs incurred in connection with the preparation of its own case.

3. Offering to help identify a third-party arbitrator, if asked by the cottagers involved, to achieve a final resolution to the dispute.

4. Offering to take any other actions that the Board believes in good faith will help resolve the dispute.

Finally, the Committee concluded that in a Private Dispute the Board will not:

1. Make any determination resolving a dispute

2. Engage in any sort of quasi-judicial action or make any sort of quasi-judicial findings

3. Make any factual findings that might be used in any sort of litigation between the disputing parties

 

 

By-Laws

of

TWILIGHT COTTAGERS

and

TWILIGHT PARK ASSOCIATION

(As Amended October 6, 2012)

Article I - Membership

(1) MEMBERSHIP - Membership in the Corporation shall be determined by the Board of Directors (the "Board") in its discretion and may include consideration of, among other things, financial capability, social compatibility, and willingness to serve in a volunteer capacity. Such determination, however, shall not be based upon the race, religion, sex or national origin of the applicant.

(a) VOTING MEMBERS - Each cottage or land lessee shall have only one vote. In the event the property is owned jointly by two or more persons, such persons shall together have a single vote and must designate the Member who votes [the "Voting Member"] on matters requiring a membership vote. Voting may be in person or by written proxy.

(b) ASSOCIATE MEMBERS -The Board may establish a class or classes of associate members for persons who are not lessees of land in Twilight Park, with such privileges and rights as the Board may determine other than the right to vote. The Board may also determine all other matters concerning associate membership including responsibilities, admission requirements and procedures and cost of associate membership.

Article II - Membership Meetings

(1) ANNUAL MEETING -The annual meeting of Members (the "Annual Meeting") shall be held on the first Saturday of August in each year for the election of directors of the Corporation, and the transaction of such other business as may come before the meeting.

(2) SPECIAL MEETINGS -Special meetings of the Members for any purpose or purposes shall be called by the Secretary upon the direction of the president or upon the written request of a majority of the Board or 25% of the Members. At a special meeting there shall be considered only such business as is specified in the notice of meeting.

(3) QUORUM -The presence in person or by proxy of a majority of the Voting Members shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business. In the absence of a quorum, a majority of the Voting Members present may adjourn the meeting and the adjourned meeting may thereafter be held without further notice.

(4) NOTICE OF MEETING -Written notice of each Annual Meeting and any special meeting shall be given to each Member at least ten (10)but not more than forty (40) days before the date of the meeting. The notice shall state the place, date and hour of the meeting and, if a special meeting, the purpose or purposes for which the meeting is being called.

Article III - Board of Directors

(1) AUTHORITY -The affairs of the Corporation shall be managed by a Board of Directors in whom all corporate powers shall be vested except as may otherwise be expressly provided by law or by the certificate of incorporation or by the Bylaws. Among the powers of the Board shall be the power to elect officers; to fill vacancies among the members of the Board or the officers of the Corporation; to hire such employees, including a superintendent, as it shall deem advisable; to make and enforce rules for the conduct of the Members and their families and guests in their use of the Corporation's property and rules for admission to membership in the Corporation or to rent property from Members; to make purchases, approve contracts and authorize indebtedness on behalf of the Corporation; to fix and collect assessments, transfer fees, membership fees and penalties; and to provide for the composition, function and authority of all committees.

(2) NOMINATION OF DIRECTORS -Nomination for the office of director may be made by:

(a) The Nominating Committee whose list of nominees shall be delivered to the Secretary not later than thirty [30] days prior to the date fixed for the election of directors; and/or

(b) A written petition signed by any five [5] Voting Members which petition shall be delivered to the Secretary not later than fifteen [15] days prior to the date fixed for the election.

The form of proxy circulated with the notice of meeting at which directors are to be elected to the Board shall be worded so as to enable the Voting Member executing the proxy to indicate his or her choice among the persons nominated, whether by the Nominating Committee or by petition.

(3) ELECTION OF DIRECTORS -The Board of Directors shall consist of nine (9) Members and they shall be divided into three classes of three (3) directors each who will hold office for a period of three years. One class shall be elected each year at the Annual Meeting. After having been elected to two consecutive three-year terms of office, a director shall be ineligible for re-election for a period of one (1) year.

(4) QUORUM -Any five (5) directors shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business, except as otherwise provided in Section (6) of this Article.

(5) TELECONFERENCE - Business transacted by the Board at a duly called meeting with a quorum present on a telephone shall be considered binding.

(6) VACANCIES - A vacancy on the Board may be filled by the vote of a majority of the directors then in office, regardless of their number. A director so elected to fill a vacancy shall serve until a successor is elected by the Voting Members at the Annual Meeting at which the former director's term would have expired and then may stand for election to two consecutive three year terms as indicated in Section (3) of this Article.

(7) NOTICE OF MEETINGS - Meetings of the Board shall be called by the President or by the Secretary upon the direction of the President or upon the request of any three (3) members of the Board. The ten (10) day notice of meeting shall be sent by mail to all directors and shall be deemed sufficient notice of any meeting.

Article IV - Officers

(1) GENERALPROVISIONS-The officers of the Corporation shall be a President, Vice President, Secretary, and Treasurer, and such other officer or officers as the Board of Directors may from time to time determine. Officers are to be elected annually by the Board to hold office until their successors are elected. Any vacancies occurring by death, resignation or removal may be filled by the Board.

The person holding the office of Vice President may at the same time be Secretary or Treasurer. The person holding the office of President may not at the same time hold any other office. The President and Vice President shall be members of the Board but no other officer need be. If an officer is not a member of the Board, such officer shall be a member ex-officio with the right to attend and participate in meetings but not to vote.

(2) POWERS AND DUTIES OF OFFICERS -The officers shall have such powers and duties as shall from time to time be determined by the Board. Unless the Board shall otherwise provide, the various officers shall have the following specific powers and duties:

(a) President: The President shall exercise general supervision and executive control over the property and affairs of the Corporation and its officers, agents and employees. He shall preside at all meetings of the Members and of the Board at which he shall be present. He shall appoint the members of all standing committees, and may appoint such special committees as he deems advisable except as limited by these By-Laws. He shall execute all contracts or agreements to which the Corporation is a party.

(b) Vice President: The Vice President shall have all the powers and duties of the President, in the absence of the President, or in the event the President is unable to act.

(c) Treasurer: The Treasurer shall be a member of the Finance and Planning Committee and have overall responsibility for recommending policy relating to the financial matters of the Corporation to the Finance and Planning Committee.

The Treasurer shall have charge of all receipts and moneys of the Corporation and shall deposit them in the name of the Corporation and disburse funds as ordered or authorized by the President, the Vice President or the Board. It shall also be his duty to collect all money due to the Corporation. He shall keep regular accounts of his receipts and disbursements, submit his records when requested by the Board or the Finance and Planning Committee and give reports as required.

(d) Secretary: The Secretary shall keep the minutes of all meetings of the Corporation and the Board, and shall notify Members and directors of their respective legally called meetings.

Article V - Limitation on Powers of the Board of Directors

(1) FIRE INSURANCE RESERVE FUND -The Board may not authorize the use of any of the moneys in the Fire Insurance Reserve Fund for any purpose other than the one for which the Fund was established without the approval of a majority of the Voting Members.

(2) LIMITATION ON INDEBTEDNESS -The Board may not, without the prior approval of a majority of the Voting Members, authorize the incurrence of aggregate indebtedness in excess of the total assessment levied at the most recent Annual Meeting and shall not incur any debt with a repayment period longer than fifteen (15) years.

(3) RESTRICTION ON THE SALE OF LAND -The Board may not authorize the disposal by sale or any other means of land belonging to the Corporation or held in the name of The Twilight Park Association ("TPA") without the prior approval of a majority of the Voting Members.

Article VI - Committees

(1) STANDING COMMITTEES

(a) Activities Committee -The Activities Committee shall consist of a Chairman, who shall serve no more than four consecutive years, and the Chairman of each activities committee authorized by Board. The Activities Committee shall be responsible to the Board for the various social, cultural and athletic activities of Twilight Park. The Activities Committee Chairman will be an ex-officio member of the Board with the right to attend and participate in meetings but not to vote.

(b) Nominating Committee -The Nominating Committee shall consist of five members, each of whom may serve no more than four consecutive years. Two members shall be appointed by the Board, and the other three members shall be appointed by the Activities Committee. The Chairman shall be appointed annually from the committee by the President. The Nominating Committee is responsible for providing nominations for directors as outlined in Article III Section (2) (a) as well as providing the Board and the Activities Committee, as directed and requested, with the nomination of candidates to positions of responsibility within the community.

(2) COMMITTEES OF THE BOARD - The President shall appoint, with Board approval. the members and the Chairmen of the following committees and any others as he and the Board shall determine:

(a) - Membership Committee

(b) - Finance and Planning Committee

(c) - Real Estate Committee

(d) - Buildings and Grounds Committee

(e) - Water and Sanitation Committee

(f) - Insurance Committee

(g) - Design Review Committee

(h) - Personnel Committee

The duties and responsibilities of each committee shall be determined by the President and the Board; however, members shall serve a maximum of two (2) consecutive three-year terms. Committee Chairmen shall serve a maximum of three (3) consecutive years as Chairman.

Article VII - Amendments

These By-laws may be amended or repealed in whole or in part only by a majority vote of the Voting Members. Written notice will be mailed to Members at least ten (10) but not more than forty (40) days prior to the date set for voting on any such proposed change in the By-laws.

Article VIII - Corporate Seal

The seal of the Corporation shall consist of a scroll seal or engraved seal containing the

name of the Corporation. The keeper of the seal shall be either the President or the Secretary.

Article IX - Indemnification

(1) ACTIONS AGAINST CERTAIN REPRESENTATIVES OF THE CORPORATION -The Corporation shall indemnify any person who is or was a director, officer, employee or agent of the Corporation against expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees), judgments, fines and amounts paid in settlement actually and reasonably incurred by him in connection with any threatened, pending or completed action, suit or proceeding, whether civil, criminal, administrative or investigative, including all appeals, to which he was, is or is threatened to be made a party by reason of the fact that he is or was such director, officer, employee or agent, provided that he acted with due care, in good faith and in a manner he reasonably believed to be in or not opposed to the best interests of the Corporation and that with respect to any criminal action or proceeding, he had no reasonable cause to believe his conduct was unlawful, and provided further that the Corporation shall have been given timely notice of such action, suit or proceeding and shall have been afforded the opportunity to participate in and defend any legal proceeding related thereto. The termination of any action, suit or proceeding by judgment, order, settlement, conviction or upon a plea of nolo contendere or its equivalent, shall not, of itself, create a presumption that the person did not act with due care, in good faith and in a manner which he reasonably believed to be in or not opposed to the best interest of the Corporation and, with respect to any criminal action or proceeding, he had no reasonable cause to believe that his conduct was unlawful.

(2) DETERMINATION OF RIGHT TO INDEMNIFICATION - Any indemnification under Section (1) of this Article shall be made by the Corporation only as authorized in the specific case upon a determination that indemnification of the director, officer, employee or agent is proper in the circumstances because he has met the applicable standards of conduct set forth in said Section (1). Such determination shall be made:

(a) by the Board by a majority vote of a quorum consisting of directors who were not parties to such action, suit or proceeding, or

(b) if such quorum is not obtainable or if the quorum of disinterested directors so directs, by independent legal counsel in a written opinion, or

(c) by a majority of the Voting Members.

(3) PREPAID EXPENSES -Expenses, including attorneys' fees, incurred in defending any action, suit, or proceeding referred to in Section (I) of this Article, may be paid by the corporation in advance of the final disposition of such action, suit, or proceeding, when authorized by the Board in such specific case, upon a written request from the director, officer, employee or agent accompanied by his agreement to repay such amount unless it shall ultimately be determined that he is entitled to be indemnified by the Corporation as authorized in this Article.

(4) OTHER RIGHTS AND REMEDIES -The indemnification provided by this Article shall not be deemed exclusive of any other rights to which those seeking indemnification may be entitled under any applicable law, agreement, vote of the Members or disinterested directors, or otherwise, both as to action in their official capacity and as to action in another capacity while holding such office, and shall continue as to a person who has ceased to be a director, officer, employee or agent and shall inure to the benefit of the heirs, executors, and administrators of such a person.

(5) INSURANCE -The Corporation may purchase and maintain insurance on behalf of any person who is or was a director, officer, employee or agent of the Corporation, against any liability asserted against him and incurred by him in any such capacity or arising out of his status as such, whether or not the Corporation would have the power to indemnify him against such liability under the provisions of this Article.

Article X - Construction and Building

External plans for new buildings and cottages or additions to existing buildings must be approved by the Board after endorsement by a Design Review Committee, but before work is begun. The Board, in addition, must approve, before work is begun, plans for all projects which alter the natural terrain and which could damage the environment. Such projects include, but are not limited to, tree-cutting, individual winter sewage and water systems, walls, culverts, and driveways.

Article XI - Maintenance and Repair

The Lessee shall, at all times during the lease term, at his or her own expense, maintain and make all necessary repairs and replacements to any structure on the leased property and to the pipes, heating system, plumbing system, window glass, fixtures and all other appliances and their appurtenances, and all equipment used on the leased property. Such maintenance, repairs and replacements, interior and exterior, ordinary as well as extraordinary, and structural as well as non-structural, shall be made promptly, as and when necessary.
