from the Webmaster   September 2006

The time has come for the yearly clean up of  the Web Page and to get ready for the coming 2007
season at Twilight. There will be new approaches to internet Twilight Information in 2007 that will
make us a more cohesive community and cut down on some of the paperwork.

John Galatti and Nora Monaco have launched an online Twilight Directory which can be modified by the
cottager to keep it up to date and makes sending emails to the entire community much easier. Listing of
Twilighters and their families will be easier to keep accurate. A link to this valuable communication facility
is listed at the top of the Twilight Web Page as "Twilight Directory". John and Nora are very helpful in its use.

2006 saw the use of PDF files by Terry Emmerich for the Snowy Owl and its following update, by Brenda Swords
for the Twilight Owl, as well as by Scott Graves for The Racket updating the tennis info and will be
probably be used by our new Twilight Owl Editor, Annie Braunstein. These files that are created by
an Adobe software package are easy to email, easy to print and have many advantages for use on the web.
To read them a free Acrobat Reader can easily be downloaded from the Internet and  installed on PC's and Macs.

  http://www.adobe.com/products/acrobat/readstep2.html

We shall certainly make sure that "snail mail" contacts are carefully maintained.

Exciting news today is that information about the 2007 Art Show and entry forms
will be available on the web from the web site of Rachel and John Roll. A link "Art Show" is listed at
the top of the Twilight Web Page. They already have done much to organize this important Twilight
activity.

Although Twilight lives and breathes twelve months a year with magnificent 
falls and great winter time activities, for many of us things start in the summer.
So it is with your web page. I'll leave on key items such as the email addresses,
Twilighters and their children , family pictures, births
weddings and deaths of 2006 and the great Snowy Owl 2006 (which I'll replace with the 2007 issue
when Terry produces it). There are many archival pictures of Twilighters on the web page now. I'd like
more and updates. The Green Book will be there in the Archival Section and will soon have the 2006
updates.

In order to make things orderly on the server where all of the 
internet information is kept, I shall take off all the old and archival material.

 I'll keep everything that has appeared for the last six plus years
on Twilight Websites (Twilight Y2k Celebration, Twilight Art Shows, 
Snowy Owls  2001-6,Twilight Owls 2001-6 and all images on my computer or on CDs. 
If any of this information is needed, it can be emailed to you.

There's a place for Artie Hommel, our hard working superintendent, whose team saved
the month of July for us all after the flood of June 26, to put information that will be useful
for everyone which we'll  have updated for 2007.

I plan to continue to list Mountain Top Web Sites. This will be helpful for checking newspaper articles,
making reservations at restaurants, contacting local merchants, etc. I'd appreciate additions to
the list, just email me.

Twilight Park Archives are in several books, private collections, family memorabilia,
Catskill mountain information, oral histories, etc. It is currently planned to gather some of these
records and at least make an electronic listing. Last year's entry in by Dean Davies on the origin of the
Twilight clubs is a start. This is a project to be shared by the whole Park and Mountain Top Community.

It remains a good policy to change the Password to logon to the Twilight Web page from
time to time. This makes information such as email addresses a little less available to the general
public. I'll always post the ID and Password on the web update notice.

 

JBP
